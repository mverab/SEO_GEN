<h2>Buying Property in Merida as a Foreigner</h2>

<h3>Foreign Ownership Laws in Mexico</h3>
<p>Foreign nationals can own property in Mexico, including in the interior regions like Merida. However, there are specific regulations and restrictions to be aware of. For instance, foreigners can directly purchase property within the "restricted zone," which extends 100 kilometers from Mexico's borders and 50 kilometers from its coastlines, through a Mexican bank trust known as a "fideicomiso."</p>

<h3>The Purchasing Process</h3>
<p>The process of buying property in Merida as a foreigner typically involves the following steps:</p>
<ol>
    <li>Obtaining a Mexican tax identification number (RFC)</li>
    <li>Opening a Mexican bank account</li>
    <li>Securing financing (if needed) through a Mexican bank or international lender</li>
    <li>Conducting due diligence on the property and reviewing legal documentation</li>
    <li>Finalizing the purchase through a public notary and registering the property</li>
